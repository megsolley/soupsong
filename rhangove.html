Hangover Soup:
The Science of It

(or, Any Soup in the Book)

Soup only postpones the reckoning, but it DOES help. Why? Because it takes away the worst of the symptoms by returning the body to a condition of anaethetization. That dry mouth, that nasty headache--they're largely the result of water loss--and soup restores the fluids.

Carbohydrates also help. They help correct the derangement of acid balance in the tissues that causes feelings of fatigue and malaise.

Bottom line--what every mother knows--when you don't feel good, eat soup. Chicken or otherwise.
