Turkish Red Lentil Soup

(Ezo Gelin Corbasi)

This rich Turkish soup sticks to the ribs and makes a great winter lunch--serve hot to 6-8 people.
  • 1 cup red lentils, washed and picked over
  • 2 Tablespoons raw rice
  • 8 cups vegetable or beef stock
  • 2 onions, finely minced
  • 4 Tablespoons butter
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 Tablespoons butter
  • 1 Tablespoon dried mint leaves, crumbled
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper or red pepper flakes
Saute the onions in 4 Tablespoons of butter in a large saucepan until they are golden brown. Add the stock, bring to a boil, add the lentils and rice, and cook until soft--about 45 minutes. At this point you can stop and store the soup as long as overnight.

When ready to serve, bring the soup to a simmer. Beat the egg yolks into the milk and stir into the simmering soup for a few minutes. Remove from heat. To serve, melt the 2 Tablespoons of butter in a small pan, add the mint leaves, and stir for a few seconds. Add the hot pepper and cook til bubbly. Stir immediately into the hot soup. Ladle into bowls and garnish each portion with a sprinkling of crushed mint.
